Brief

My team and I immersed ourselves in the world of eSports to incorporate more engaging features on Upcomer app to grow their eSport fanbase and create a strong sense of community.

View Prototype

Overview

The Challenge

Upcomer is a companion app for eSports fans get informed about their favorite eSports teams and important events but often leave after finding score information they want.

Our biggest challenge was to seamlessly implement engaging features that resulted in users interacting with the app and the Upcomer community longer without obstructing their initial purpose for visiting Upcomer.

Our Process

Research

Simply put, Electronic sports is organized competition where teams face off against one another playing popular multiplayer video games. This form of entertainment has blown up in recent year and professional gamers are watched worldwide by millions of viewers, who attend live events or use streaming services like Twitch to watch.

What is eSports impact?

To our surprise we found that eSports has been strategically structured to mimic traditional athletic sports in almost every way. This insight led us to understand eSports from a more familiar scope and would later influence our feature decisions in our ideation process.

Key Takeaways:

  • According to Business Insider, 62% of US eSports viewers are between the ages of 18-34.

  • It's anticipated that global eSports sponsorship revenue will reach $359 million in 2018, up 53% year-over-year

  • Data suggests that Gen Zers are more receptive to eSports compared to traditional sports.

What is on the Upcomer app?

I conducted a content audit that would help us understand the range of information and functionality the app currently offers it users.

To my concern, most of the content was only accessible via the home screen causing the app to be at least 4-5 levels deep. It was clear that my team would have to make the app more robust navigation and spread out the content in a way that is easier for users to access information.

How do users navigate Upcomer?

We conducted usability tests with several gamers and non-gamers to observe a wider range user pain points.

We observed that all users had difficulty navigating through the app because the nav bar disappeared when they click on different types of content which lead them either exiting the app or tapping the back arrow upwards of 10 times before returning to the screen they started on.

Who makes up the eSports community?

To gain a better understanding of our target audience, we interviewed several types of "gamers". These interviews taught us about the frustrations, motivations, and goals of Upcomers' potential userbase.

Key Takeaways:

  • Improving ones game is a large reason people look up highlights and join eSports forums

  • Users enjoyed gaming as a way to gather and bond with friends

  • Conversely, trolls are very real in the gaming community so gamers cautiously stick to their primary circle or utilize friends gaming circles to fill out a team

What are trends among users?

To synthesize the data from our user interviews and identify key trends that existed in the existing marketplace, we collaborated via affinity mapping to find patterns among gamer/eSport fans.

We realized that there were 2 opportunities to improve the existing app by:

  • Providing references for users to improve their own game and continually come back on the app

  • Encourage more interaction between users on posts, events, and with each other.

What features do we tackle?

We began brainstorming features that could would fit into our observed areas of opportunity found in our affinity mapping. We landed on creating the following features which held high user value at a low level of implementation:

  • Easily accessible game tips & strategies for users to reference

  • Providing live event, player, and team scores and statistics.

  • In app messaging and sharing content with friends

Forming a Persona

To help my team understand the needs and goals of our users, I synthesized our data and created our user persona, Xander. In many ways our persona helped us come to united decisions based off of the wants and needs of users instead of making decisions based off of our assumptions as designers.

Designing Solutions


Ideation

I led the ideation process of the redesign focused on a collaborative team effort. Together my team was able to share ideas in design studios and come to united decisions based on our user research. We also utilized paper prototyping to test our ideas early and often before committing to a final design.

Final Designs


News Screen

  • Customizable news feed based on a users favorites with the option to see all trending eSports news.

  • Users are able to hide and show spoilers and set alarms for events with just a tap.

  • Quick access and editing users favorite teams, players, and games.

Profile and Team Screens

  • Robust editing/adding of favorites and friends.

  • Central hub for saved content that can be easily accessed.

  • Personalization of background images, time zone, and view gamer tags to connect via console.

Strategies Screen

  • Engaging users via liking, commenting, saving, and in app sharing features.

  • Simplified filtering by game for easier access to desired content.

  • Dedicated screen for improving users gaming by providing match highlights and useful tips.

Reflection

It was amazing immersing myself in the eSports world and understand the passionate people of the community. In retrospect, I would've loved to do some contextual inquiries with users watching a game together, or better yet, visit a tournament to capture the eSports environment and have conversations with a more diverse spectrum of eSports fans. I also think conducting A/B testing with our icons early on would have saved us from multiple navigation and feature iterations early on.

Next Steps

We tested the High Fidelity Prototype with several users, both gamers and people with little knowledge on the eSports industry to test out how intuitive we made the app, especially for users new to the gaming industry.

  • In order to make more changes and iterations, we would need to test the app with more users, gain more feedback, and make iterations from there.

  • I would like to continue to roll out features that my team ideated early on in the process, such as adding a Fantasy League feature (similar to fantasy football). We found that the gaming community is really engaged, and several of our users want more ways to interact with their friends as well as the professional eSports circuit.
