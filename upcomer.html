Overview

The Challenge

Users come to Upcomer to get informed about their favorite eSports teams and games, but often leave after finding the information they want. This is troublesome for the lifespan of an app that builds itself upon community and growth. Our biggest challenge was to seamlessly implement engaging features that results in users interacting on the app longer without obstructing their initial purpose for visiting Upcomer.

The Solution

Our Process

Our process relied heavily in understanding the eSports as business as well as empathizing with the enthusiastic eSports community.

What is eSports?

In a nutshell, electronic sports (aka eSports) is organized competition where teams face off against one another playing popular multiplayer video games. This form of entertainment has blown up in recent year and professional gamers are watched worldwide by millions of viewers, who attend live events or use streaming services like Twitch to watch.

eSports has been structured to mimic traditional athletic sports which creates familiarity with viewers whether or not they are gamers

What is on the Upcomer app?

I conducted a content/feature audit that would help us understand the range of content and functionality on the app. Additionally, I also created a visual breakdown of content hierarchy found in each main nav.

To my concern, most of the content was stored on the homepage causing it to be at least 4-5 levels deep. It became apparent that deciding what content to keep might prove difficult later in our design phase.

How do users think about navigate Upcomer?

We conducted usability tests with several gamers and non-gamers to observe a wide range pain points. We observed that users had difficulty navigating through the app because the navigation bar disappeared when they click on content finding themselves having to exit the app or tap the back arrow several times before returning to the screen they started on.

Who makes up the eSports community?

To gain a better understanding of our target audience, we interviewed several self described gamers. These interviews taught us about the frustrations, motivations, and goals of our target users.

After all of our eSports research of the eSports world we felt confidently that we could execute interviews and recieve meaningful insights of a variety of eSports fans. We curated an interview guide that sought out people who are avid gamers and/or eSports viewers.

Improving ones game is a large reason people look up highlights and join eSports forums

Users enjoyed gaming as a way to gather and bond with friends

Conversely, trolls are very real in the gaming community so gamers cautiously stick to their primary circle or utilize friends gaming circles to fill out a team

What are trends among users?

After our interviews we utilized affinity mapping to find patterns among gamer/eSport fans expectations for an eSports companion app. From here we selected features based on task complexity and value to users. We concluded that the most beneficial features for users would be concentrated on content discovery, social engagement, and user customization.

Forming a Persona

Xander realizes he's only one victory away from leveling up to a platinum portrait but he can't defeat his last opponent. He remembers watching an eSports match where the LA Gladiators won using a special kill tactic that would definitely help him out.

Xander quickly opens Upcomer and searches "LA Gladiators”. He scrolls on the team profile and looks at promising posts here and there, but can't find any video highlights. Designing Solutions

Ideation

I suggested we make a succinct wireframe guide so the entire team could collaborate on rapid wire-framing and conduct usability tests of our designs immediately with paper prototypes to find the friction points early in the design process.

We discovered that users were confused about the saving functionality and iconography and they stated desires for a rating system on news and strategy feeds. This lead to further iterations and tweaks that improved our overall design.

Final Designs

News Screen

  • Customizable news feed based on a users favorites with the option to see all trending eSports news.

  • Users are able to hide and show spoilers and set alarms for events with just a tap.

  • Quick access and editing users favorite teams, players, and games.

Profile and Team Screens

  • Robust editing/adding of favorites and friends.

  • Central hub for saved content that can be easily accessed.

  • Personalization of background images, time zone, and view gamer tags to connect via console.

Strategies Screen

  • Engaging users via liking, commenting, saving, and in app sharing features.

  • Simplified filtering by game for easier access to desired content.

  • Dedicated screen for improving users gaming by providing match highlights and useful tips.

The Results

New User Flow

Quantitative Results:

Before, users spent as little as 7 seconds on the site before bouncing to an affiliate link. In final usability tests, users spent between 3-5 minutes browsing the site and completing their purchase.

Qualitative Results:

"Site looks clean and simple with smooth a navigation."

"A simple process, I know where I can find items without thinking about it."

"I like the 'Finish the Look'...it helps me be a stylist without having a stylist."

What's really changed?

When comparing Choisi's current structure, our revised site map now provides a robust navigation schema that provides users with various pathways to browse, purchase, and gather inspiration from products. The design also allows many avenues for users to shop whether it's via designer, Instagram, or item type.

We saw this new structures success in action with our new user flow in which users were never taken to an external site and can easily accomplish purchasing products in just minutes.

Key Takeaways

It was amazing immersing myself in the eSports world and understand the passionate people of the community. In retrospect, I would've loved to do some contextual inquiries with users watching a game together, or better yet, visit a tournament to capture the eSports environment and have conversations with a more diverse spectrum of eSports fans. I also think conducting A/B testing with our icons early on would have saved us from multiple navigation and feature iterations early on.
