Overview

The Challenge

I identified two major concerns with Burger Records site:

1. Their unconventional navigation and pages overloaded with content creates an unpleasant user experience.

2. Currently, they use two different independent companies to handle their e-Commerce functions causing a confusing user flow.

The Solution

Our Process

The key to our UX process with Choisi was to clearly define the project scope with the goal of creating feasible solutions based in strong user research. In our meeting with the founder, Sherry, we discovered that their AI technology hadn't been launched because of Squarespace limitations. Understanding this, we decided to deliver a redesign that Sherry's startup team could confidently implement ASAP to their current site to potentially grow their user base while they developed their relaunch.

Research

An initial run through of Choisi's site presented a huge friction point: no e-commerce functionality. While the site did have a curated group of global designers for users to select from, it lacked any way to browse, filter, or purchase products without redirecting to external designer sites. The lack of engaging e-commerce functionality became our hypothesis as to why users were bouncing from the site at such high rates.

User Flow

Where are the problem areas?

My first step in this phase was to experience the Burger Records site for myself in order to fully identify its function(s). I discovered that the site primarily serves as an e-commerce experience, however, it is also used as a demo submission platform, social media hub, and for event discovery. This site layout and structure differs drastically from typical e-commerce practices that drastically affected users learnability, memorability, and overall satisfaction (and dare I say their delight as well).

How is the site structured?

Who is the competition?

When comparing Choisi to their competitors, we found consistent content, features, and themes across the board. This method helped frame the structure and aesthetic in our redesign phase.

Common Structures and Features: Categories/filters, latest trends, wish lists, featured products, styling advice, product details, highlighted designers.

Common Visuals: Large amounts of white space with an emphasis on colorful fashion shots.

How do users navigate currently?

To fully validate our hypothesis we observed users as they attempted to complete either Task #1 or Task #2.

As it turns out, user were just as frustrated as us when navigating simple tasks. In fact, users spent most of their time being redirected and navigating their way farther from the Choisi site altogether.

One user even stated, “Why would I even bother with Choisi if I just ended up on Etsy and could buy the product there?”

Product Discovery Features

"Editor’s Picks": We re-labeled and redesigned the site’s current “Top 10 Lists” to “Editor’s Picks," a small change that strengthens Choisi's credibility for users like Elise who values authentic brands.

"Designer Spotlight": My team saw a lot of opportunity to promote user discovery by highlighting designers right on the homepage. Users are then taken to designer pages where they can view full collections and purchase available products without leaving Choisi's site.

"Shoppers Also Viewed/You May like": To further increase user engagement we added components that encourages users to explore what other users view and provide product suggestions in the checkout flow for any last minute purchases.
