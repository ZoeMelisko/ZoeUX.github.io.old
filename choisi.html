Brief

Choisi is a global fashion boutique utilizing AI technology to help users discover emerging luxury designer brands base on the users online shopping habits.

View Prototype

Overview

The Challenge

Choisi's founder came to us with two main problems:

1. 60% of users are bouncing from Choisi almost immediately after visiting the site.

2. Choisi's AI technology can't be implemented due to their 3rd party site hosting so they need personalization features that will complement the AI once it's launched.


The Solution

Deliver a full site redesign in a 2.5 week sprint focused on e-commerce functionality and incorporating personalization features to keep users engaged on Choisi.

Our Process

The key to our UX process with Choisi was to clearly define the project scope with the goal of creating feasible solutions based in strong user research. In our meeting with the founder, Sherry, we discovered that their AI technology hadn't been launched because of Squarespace limitations. Understanding this, we decided to deliver a redesign that Sherry's startup team could confidently implement ASAP to their current site to potentially grow their user base while they developed their relaunch.

Research

An initial run through of Choisi's site presented a huge friction point: no e-commerce functionality. While the site did have a curated group of global designers for users to select from, it lacked any way to browse, filter, or purchase products without redirecting to external designer sites. The lack of engaging e-commerce functionality became our hypothesis as to why users were bouncing from the site at such high rates.

Where are the problem areas?

Conducting a heuristic evaluation was essential for my team to document components of the site that might cause a users pain points at a broad scale. From this we found several areas to improve user recognition and efficiency, primarily regarding the navigation and consistent site redirects.

What do fashion consumers say?

In Choisi's current state real-world users were essentially non-existent. This caused my team to pivot, deciding to interview a variety of potential users who are frequent fashion consumers ranging in age, income, and shopping habits.

Interviewees had mixed experiences with the luxury fashion world but when it came to online shopping all largely agreed that products should be easy to browse/find, details of product/designer are valuable, and personal customer service goes a long way.

Who is the competition?

When comparing Choisi to their competitors, we found consistent content, features, and themes across the board. This method helped frame the structure and aesthetic in our redesign phase.

Common Structures and Features: Categories/filters, latest trends, wish lists, featured products, styling advice, product details, highlighted designers.

Common Visuals: Large amounts of white space with an emphasis on colorful fashion shots.

How do users navigate Choisi currently?

To fully validate our hypothesis we observed users as they attempted to complete either Task #1 or Task #2.

As it turns out, user were just as frustrated as us when navigating simple tasks. In fact, users spent most of their time being redirected and navigating their way farther from the Choisi site altogether.

One user even stated, “Why would I even bother with Choisi if I just ended up on Etsy and could buy the product there?”

What are trends among users?

During our affinity mapping session, we organized our user and market research data. Our users advocated for common e-commerce elements, calling for an easy process but they also showed interest in more complex features like Insta shop integration or real-time style advice.

What features do we tackle?

With clear patterns formed from our affinity map, we began prioritizing features based on a "Value vs Effort" axis. I wanted to focus on features users will interact with frequently while considering the effort that Choisi need to implement.

Once we settled on the effort and value of our features, I encouraged my team to further focus the features into primary and secondary brackets to ensure we deliver a product with a strong e-Commerce foundation.

Wait...who are our users again?

Keeping our design goals centered on our users was a huge priority to my team, we collaborated on two personas:

Celeste the “Inspo Seeker/High Spender," frequently purchases luxury pieces that inspire her. She's frustrated when a site lacks style inspiration or if purchasing process takes too long.

Elise the “Investment Spender,” occasionally invests in luxury pieces after researching brands/designers/product details. She's loyal to high-quality, ethically sourced brands and is frustrated when she can't find products up to her standards.

Empathizing with the user

To visualize Celeste's current Choisi experience I created a Journey Map of her thoughts and emotions at specific touchpoints while completing the following scenario:

Celeste is preparing for her biggest career opportunity yet as a judge for the LA Food Fest. She can’t find anything inspiring enough in her closet for such an important event. Her goal is to use this press opportunity to spotlight a unique emerging designer.

Designing Solutions

Ideation

Our ideation process began with rough sketching and a collaborative design studio with the founder, Sherry, to share our design thinking. Our users told us that if they’re going to buy luxury products, they expect the experience to reflect luxury via high-end design. We accomplished this by focusing on a clean, editorial feel with large, lifestyle images, white space that elevates Choisi's online presence. After establishing a fundamental style we conducted several rounds of A/B testing with different layouts to explore the best content hierarchy for users.

Final Designs

Improved Structure

We took a standard approach when incorporating e-commerce components, creating an intuitive user flow was the main priority of my team. We designed a familiar navigation structure and robust filtering options by designer, style, and even occasion for users in similar scenarios as our persona Celeste.

Smaller details such as breadcrumbs, "Sort by" categories, and page view options may be overlooked by users but serve essential purposes for the user to easily accomplish their tasks.


Product Discovery Features

"Editor’s Picks": We re-labeled and redesigned the site’s current “Top 10 Lists” to “Editor’s Picks," a small change that strengthens Choisi's credibility for users like Elise who values authentic brands.

"Designer Spotlight": My team saw a lot of opportunity to promote user discovery by highlighting designers right on the homepage. Users are then taken to designer pages where they can view full collections and purchase available products without leaving Choisi's site.

"Shoppers Also Viewed/You May like": To further increase user engagement we added components that encourages users to explore what other users view and provide product suggestions in the checkout flow for any last minute purchases.

Personalization Features

"Finish the Look": On product detail pages, users can get suggestions on what goes with the item they’re looking at so they can build an entire look vs. just buying an individual piece. This adds a sense of personal styling without heavy duty AI technology.

"Shop the Look": Provides users with head to toe looks that they can purchase as a complete stylish outfit or as a separate that they can envision working with their own clothes based of the outfit shown.


The Results

Prototype

Revised Site Map

New User Flow

Quantitative Results:

Before, users spent as little as 7 seconds on the site before bouncing to an affiliate link. In final usability tests, users spent between 3-5 minutes browsing the site and completing their purchase.

Qualitative Results:

Users describe the new experience positively across the board.

"Site looks clean and simple with smooth a navigation."

"A simple process, I know where I can find items without thinking about it."

"I like the 'Finish the Look'...it helps me be a stylist without having a stylist."

What's really changed?

When comparing Choisi's current structure, our revised site map now provides a robust navigation schema that provides users with various pathways to browse, purchase, and gather inspiration from products. The design also allows many avenues for users to shop whether it's via designer, Instagram, or item type.

We saw this new structures success in action with our new user flow in which users were never taken to an external site and can easily accomplish purchasing products in just minutes.

Key Takeaways

Small changes make all the difference: In this project I realized that the simplest components like "Sort by" options are empowering tools for users to create a better experience for themselves.

Not all feedback is valuable feedback: Without real-world users we relied on interviewees and usability feedback from outside our key demographic. We really had to evaluate the worth of changing and/or adding features based on user feedback that didn't necessarily align with the luxury fashion space.


Next Steps

  • Continue to test site functionality with more users, specifically more luxury product shoppers.

  • Build out additional features like a shop insta page and user-generated ‘editor’s picks’ to increase participation and time spent on site.
